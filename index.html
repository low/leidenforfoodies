Leiden for Foodies

Food & Drink guide for people visiting Leiden, the Netherlands.

So you came to beautiful Leiden and you’re wondering where to have a bite to eat or enjoy a nice drink. You should check out these places.

Bistro Bord’o

Relatively new in Leiden, this French-style restaurant is staffed by young talents. They have a small menu, but with flavours like these, you don’t need much choice. Excellent wines and fair prices, too. Highly recommended.

  • Apothekersdijk 2
  • bistrobordo.nl
  • Closed on Tuesday

Victor Russo’s Osteria

Proper authentic Italian food, made by Victor, who personally imports top quality fresh produce from Italy. No pizzas or lasagnas here, but rustic seasonal dishes. Oh, and his wines are the best in town. Nom.

  • Vrouwensteeg 16
  • victorrusso.nl/osteria
  • Closed on Monday

Burgerzaken

Beautiful art-deco-like restaurant and café, that does fab lunches as well as dinner, in a Mediterranean style. Usually there’s a good selection of vegetarian dishes here, too.

  • Breestraat 123
  • restaurantburgerzaken.nl

Verboden Toegang

Simple but good food at this themed restaurant. Don’t expect to be amazed, but if you fancy some steak and chips, you can’t go wrong here.

  • Kaiserstraat 7
  • verbodentoegang.nl

Dartel

If you truly want to splurge, go to Dartel. French style haute cuisine, the best in town. Not sporting a Michelin star at the moment, but they might as well be. Exquisite.

  • Kloksteeg 13
  • restaurantdartel.nl
  • Closed on Sunday

Woo Ping

Has been in Leiden forever, and serves some of the best Chinese food in town. But be sure to ask for their dishes to be made in traditional Chinese style (or ask for Sandra and mention “116” to her), or else you might get Dutch-Chinese grub, which isn’t nearly as tasty.

  • Diefsteeg 15
  • wooping-leiden.nl

Sushi Bento

Small Japanese style “restaurantlet” where they make really good sushi. Everything is prepared on the spot, so freshness guaranteed. Eat in or take-away. The latter takes a while, though.

  • Haarlemmerstraat 191
  • sushibento.eu
  • Closed on Tuesday

Iboe Tjilik

Tiny Indonesian take-away near the iconic De Valk windmill, with one single table if you want to eat in. However, totally worth it, as their beef rendang is unparalleled. As an alternative, try Bungamas (Janvossensteeg 61), which isn’t half bad either.

  • Lammermarkt 9
  • Closed on Monday

Noroc

Great little place to have lunch.

  • Pieterskerk-Choorsteeg 4
  • eetwinkelnoroc.nl
  • Closed on Monday

Café van Engelen

Good coffee, free wifi, a decent lunch, and seating outside over a bridge. Their classic Berliner sandwich is a personal favourite.

  • Nieuwe Rijn 1
  • cafevanengelen.nl

’t Suppiershuysinghe

Cozy coffee house situated at one of the oldest and most beautiful little squares in Leiden.

  • Gerecht 2
  • koffiehuisje.com

De Bonte Koe

Old-fashioned Bar/Café with excellent beers and snacks (try a Tripel Karmeliet and a smoked sausage). And if you don’t fancy a drink, at least take a look inside. You won’t be disappointed.

  • Hooglandsekerkkoorsteeg 13
  • cafedebontekoe.net/english

De Twee Spieghels

Jazzcafé near the Leiden Castle that also happens to serve very decent wines. There’s live music regularly.

  • Nieuwstraat 11
  • detweespieghels.nl

Meneer Jansen

Bar. My local. Nuff said.

  • Nieuwe Rijn 21
  • cafemeneerjansen.nl
