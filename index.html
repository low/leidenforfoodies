Leidenforfoodies

Food and Drinks guide for people visiting Leiden, the Netherlands

Hi! I’m Lodewijk “Low” Schutte. I’m a foodie and lived in Leiden all my life. If you’re from out of town and you’re looking to have lunch, dinner or just a couple of drinks in my home town, the list below can help you along.

Bistro Bord’o

Relatively new in Leiden, this French-style restaurant is staffed by young talents. They have a small menu, but with tastes like this, you don’t need much choice. Excellent wines and affordable, too. Highly recommended.

Apothekersdijk 2
www.bistrobordo.nl
Closed on Tuesday

Victor Russo’s Osteria

Proper authentic Italian food, made by Victor, who personally imports top quality fresh produce from Italy. No pizzas or lasagnas here, but vibrant in season rustic dishes. Oh, and his wines are the best in town. Nom.

Vrouwensteeg 16
www.victorrusso.nl/osteria
Closed on Monday & Tuesday

Burgerzaken

Beautiful art-deco-like restaurant and café, that does fab lunches as well as dinner, in a Mediterranean style. Usually there’s a good selection of vegetarian dishes here, too.

Breestraat 123
www.restaurantburgerzaken.nl

Verboden Toegang

Simple but good food at this themed restaurant. Don’t expect to be amazed, but if you fancy some steak and chips, you can’t go wrong here.

Kaiserstraat 7
www.verbodentoegang.nl

Dartel

If you truly want to splurge, go to Dartel. French style haute cuisine, the best in town. Not sporting a Michelin star at the moment, but they might as well be. Exquisite.

Kloksteeg 13
www.restaurantdartel.nl
Closed on Sunday

Woo Ping

Has been in Leiden forever, and serves some of the best Chinese food in town. But be sure to ask for their dishes “in traditional Chinese style” (or ask for Sandra and mention “116” to her), or else you might get Dutch-Chinese grub, which isn’t nearly as tasty.

Diefsteeg 15
www.wooping-leiden.nl

Sushi Bento

Small Japanese style “restaurantlet” where they make really good sushi. Everything is prepared on the spot, so freshness guaranteed. You can eat in, but they don’t have a liquor license, so no alcohol is served.

Haarlemmerstraat 191
www.sushibento.eu
Closed on Tuesday

Iboe Tjilik

Tiny Indonesian take-away (near the iconic De Valk windmill), with one single table if you want to eat in. However, totally worth it, as their beef rendang is unparalleled. As an alternative, try Surakarta (Noordeinde 51-53), which isn’t half bad either.

Lammermarkt 9
Closed on Monday

Noroc

Great little place to have lunch.

Pieterskerk-Choorsteeg 4
www.eetwinkelnoroc.nl
Closed on Monday

Bagels & Beans

Surprisingly good coffee in this quirky franchise lunchroom. And tasty bagels as well, although they do toast them, so a guy from New York I know may not agree.

Haarlemmerstraat 38 or Maarsmansteeg 8
www.bagelsbeans.nl

De Bonte Koe

Old-fashioned Bar/Café with excellent beers and snacks (try a Tripel Karmeliet and a smoked sausage). And if you don’t fancy a drink, at least take a look inside. You won’t be disappointed.

Hooglandsekerkkoorsteeg 13
www.cafedebontekoe.net/english

De Twee Spieghels

Jazzcafé near the Leiden Castle that also happens to serve very decent wines. There’s live music regularly.

Nieuwstraat 11
www.detweespieghels.nl

Meneer Jansen

Bar. My local. Nuff said.

Nieuwe Rijn 21
www.cafemeneerjansen.nl
